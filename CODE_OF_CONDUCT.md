# Edusteps Hub Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as members, contributors, maintainers, and community leaders of Edusteps Hub, pledge to make participation in our community a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone.

We are committed to fostering an open, inclusive, diverse, and supportive environment regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, nationality, race, religion, gender identity, gender expression, sexual orientation, level of experience, education, or socio-economic status.

Our goal is to create a healthy and collaborative community where everyone can learn, contribute, and grow together.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- Treating others with kindness, empathy, and respect
- Being constructive when giving feedback
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences
- Accepting responsibility for mistakes and learning from them
- Supporting and encouraging new contributors
- Prioritizing the success and well-being of the community

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal attacks or inflammatory behavior
- Publishing private information without explicit permission
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted advances
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or educational environment

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers and community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards.

They may take appropriate corrective actions in response to behavior deemed harmful, offensive, threatening, or otherwise inconsistent with this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code contributions, issues, discussions, and other interactions that violate these guidelines.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Edusteps Hub community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories
- Issue trackers
- Pull requests
- Discussions and forums
- Social media channels
- Community events
- Any public or private communication representing the project

Individuals representing Edusteps Hub are expected to uphold these standards at all times.

---

## Reporting Violations

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers.

When reporting an incident, please provide:

- A description of the behavior
- Relevant screenshots, logs, or evidence
- Information about when and where the incident occurred

All reports will be reviewed fairly, confidentially, and promptly.

Project maintainers are committed to protecting the privacy and security of anyone reporting misconduct.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders may apply the following actions depending on the severity and frequency of violations.

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**

Minor violations or unintentional behavior that negatively affects the community.

**Consequence**

A private warning explaining the issue and guidance on how to avoid similar behavior in the future.

---

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**

Repeated violations or disruptive conduct.

**Consequence**

A formal warning and temporary restrictions on participation if necessary.

Continued violations may result in stronger enforcement actions.

---

### 3. Temporary Suspension

**Community Impact**

Serious violations of community standards.

**Consequence**

Temporary suspension from project discussions, contributions, or community participation for a specified period.

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**

Severe or repeated violations, harassment, discrimination, threats, or other harmful conduct.

**Consequence**

Permanent removal from all Edusteps Hub community spaces and activities.

---

## Commitment to Education and Collaboration

Edusteps Hub is built to support learning, innovation, and open-source collaboration.

We encourage respectful discussion, knowledge sharing, mentorship, and constructive feedback that helps contributors improve their skills and make meaningful contributions.
